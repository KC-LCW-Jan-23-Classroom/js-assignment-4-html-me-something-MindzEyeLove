So, What Is A Blockchain Anyway?

In simple terms, a blockchain is a public ledger that records bits(blocks) of information and stores them within a series(chain) of nodes(computers) in a peer to peer network. This differs from traditional information sharing techniques primarily due to is decentralized nature. This means that the entire network history is recorded and stored transparently by individual people(more accuratley, individual nodes), instead of centralized, privatized, corporate data centers. So, What Is ₿itcoin?

₿itcoin(₿TC) is a peer to peer digital cash with instantaneous transaction settlement. It operates without the need for a third party intermediary and all transactions are stored on a public ledger, aka a blockchain. It became known as a "cryptocurrency" because the transactions are secured and verified by using advanced cryptography. ₿itcoin has more recently become one of the leading forms of "high time-preference" store of value due to the fact that there are only 21 million ₿TC available. This is hard-coded into the protocol. Currently, approximately 19 million have already been mined, and it is estimated that the last one will be mined around the year 2140. In the financial context, censorship resistance is the ability to carry out financial actions despite the wishes of any third party. In crypto, the three pillars of censorship resistance are:
